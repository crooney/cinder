Bounce Example

Explained below.

This example drmonstrates how markup may be generated to make short work of fairly complex SVG.

bouncer = do
                root >>= insert circ
                root >>= byTag "circle" >>= stagger "cx" 50 100
                root >>= byTag "animate" >>= zipInsert fx >>= stagger "begin" 0 2
                return ()
                where
                    circ = foldl1 (!+) $ map go colors
                    go x = cRXY 25 50 100 ! fill x !+ aADR "cy" 2.0 1 ! fill "freeze"
                                ! begin "indefinite" !< Complete
                    fx = [bounce 0.4 3 100 400, settle 0.35 3 100 400,
                          bounce 0.75 4 100 400, settle 0.65 4 100 400]
                    colors = ["lightpink", "lightblue", "lightsalmon", "lightgreen"]

go marries a simple circle definition to a color and then sets it to animate its vertical position (cy), without specifying how it should animate. The resulting four circles are then inserted into the document's root Node. A list of the circle Nodes is retrieved and their horizontal positions (cx) staggered to make a row. A list of Markup, created by effects functions bounce and settle, is applied to the animation Nodes and their start times staggered. And that's it.

The expansion is similar but works on the radius (r) attribute:

grower = do
                root >>= byTag "animate" >>= mapM_ deleteSelf
                root >>= byTag "circle" >>= zipInsert an
                root >>= byTag "animate" >>= stagger "begin" 9 0.75
                return ()
                where
                    an = zipWith (!+) (repeat (aADR "r" 0.75 1 ! fill "freeze"))
                                        [bounce 0.5 5 25 50, settle 0.45 5 25 50,
                                         bounce 0.85 6 25 50, settle 0.75 6 25 50]
